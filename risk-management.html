The Problem

Why Traditional Risk Programs Fail

Most organizations have a risk register — but few have a functional risk management program. The gap between documenting risks and actually reducing them is where breaches happen.

🔴
No Financial Context for Risk Decisions
Security teams use qualitative ratings like "High / Medium / Low" that mean nothing to finance or the board. Without dollar figures attached to risks, security investments compete poorly for budget against revenue-generating initiatives — and security loses.
🔴
Inability to Communicate Risk to the Board
60% of CISOs report they cannot effectively communicate cyber risk in terms the board understands. Heat maps and traffic lights don't translate into capital allocation decisions. Boards need dollars and probabilities — not colors and feelings.
🔴
Static, Manual Risk Registers
Traditional risk registers are built once a year in a spreadsheet, then ignored. They don't update when your environment changes, new vulnerabilities emerge, or threat actor activity escalates — making them dangerously outdated within weeks of creation.
🔴
Third-Party & Supply Chain Risk Blindness
45% of significant breaches now involve a third party. Yet most risk programs rely on annual vendor questionnaires completed by vendors themselves — with no continuous monitoring, no technical validation, and no real-time risk visibility into your supply chain.
