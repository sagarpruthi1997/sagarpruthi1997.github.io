The Problem

Why GRC Programs Fail

Most compliance programs are built around audit cycles — not continuous risk management. That creates predictable, avoidable failures that cost organizations millions in remediation, regulatory fines, and reputational damage.

🗂️
Audit Scrambles
Compliance teams spend 3–6 months before each audit frantically collecting evidence, correcting gaps, and preparing documentation that should have been maintained year-round. The cycle repeats indefinitely with no lasting improvement.
🔁
Multi-Framework Redundancy
Organizations subject to SOC 2, ISO 27001, and HIPAA maintain three separate programs, three separate teams, and three separate sets of evidence — 80% of which overlap. This is a massive waste of skilled security talent.
📅
Point-in-Time Compliance Illusions
Passing an annual audit does not mean you are compliant for the remaining 364 days. Continuous changes to systems, personnel, and configurations create compliance drift almost immediately after the auditor leaves.
📁
Policy & Control Management Chaos
Policies live in SharePoint folders. Controls are tracked in spreadsheets. Evidence is emailed. This fragmented approach makes it impossible to demonstrate compliance efficiently and creates catastrophic blind spots.
