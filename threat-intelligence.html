The Problem

Why Most Teams Fly Blind

Threat intelligence exists in abundance. What organizations lack is the ability to process it at speed, filter it for relevance, and operationalize it before the attack window closes. Raw data is not intelligence — actionable, contextualized, timely signals are.

🌊
Intelligence Overload
Security teams drown in raw threat data — millions of IOCs, CVEs, and threat reports per day — with no way to identify which threats are relevant to their specific environment, technology stack, or industry vertical. Volume is the enemy of action.
❓
Lack of Context
A list of malicious IPs means nothing without context. Without knowing which threats target your industry, your technology stack, and your geography, intelligence is just noise that consumes analyst time without improving your security posture.
🔔
Reactive vs. Proactive Security
Most organizations react to threats after they arrive — after the phishing campaign has landed, after the vulnerability is being exploited, after credentials appear for sale. Without proactive intelligence, there is no opportunity to implement defenses while time remains.
🕳️
Dark Web Blindness
Criminal forums, paste sites, and dark web marketplaces contain your stolen credentials, planned attacks against your organization, and leaked data — but most organizations have zero visibility into these spaces where attacks are planned and sold before execution.
